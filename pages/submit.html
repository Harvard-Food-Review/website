The first historic solicit via Twitter... the rules have stayed pretty much the same.

The Harvard Food Review takes submissions in the following form:
* Finished pieces should be attached as Microsoft Word documents in an email to harvardfoodreview[at]gmail.com. *
* Pitches should be emailed to harvardfoodreview[at]gmail.com. *

We are looking for:
* personal essays *
* well-researched nonfiction *
* profiles and interviews *
* short stories *
* recipes *
* art, including photos *
* mystery option... surprise us! *
on anything and everything epicurean.

This can be stuff like: why your grandma only eats kimchi in the morning,
an interview with Jeremy Allen White from The Bear,
the time you consumed your first popsicle,
asking a political candidate what foods they eat in a day,
etc.

We are bored by:
extremely fluffy fluff pieces
confusing stories that should be left for your therapist
plagiarism
gender-coded language
racist takes on cultural food

Sadly and delectably, restaurant review content is reserved exclusively for our Staff Writers on assignment.
